History of India



Ancient India (Before 500 BCE)

Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2500–1500 BCE): One of the world's oldest urban civilizations, known for planned cities like Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa.

Vedic Period (c. 1500–500 BCE): Aryans migrated to India, composing the Vedas, the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism. Early roots of Hindu society and caste system.





Classical India (500 BCE – 1200 CE)

Maurya Empire (321–185 BCE): Founded by Chandragupta Maurya; under Ashoka, it became one of the largest empires and promoted Buddhism.

Gupta Empire (c. 320–550 CE): Known as the Golden Age of India — advancements in mathematics, astronomy, literature, and art.

Rise of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism as dominant religions.





Medieval India (1200–1700 CE)

Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526): Islamic rule began with rulers like Qutub-ud-din Aibak and Alauddin Khilji.

Mughal Empire (1526–1857): Founded by Babur, flourished under rulers like Akbar, Shah Jahan (who built the Taj Mahal), and Aurangzeb.

Rich in architecture, arts, and culture. Hindu-Muslim synthesis in music, language, and cuisine.





Colonial India (1757–1947)

British East India Company gained power after the Battle of Plassey (1757).

1857 Revolt: First major uprising against British rule.

British Raj (1858–1947): Direct control by the British Crown.





Modern India (Post-1947)

Independence (1947): India gained freedom from British rule on August 15, 1947.

Republic (1950): Constitution adopted on January 26, 1950, making India a sovereign democratic republic.

Since then, India has grown as a secular, multi-lingual, multi-religious democracy, and is now one of the world’s leading economies and technological hubs.
